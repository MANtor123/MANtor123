Historic places in the V&A Waterfront

Clock Tower

Clock Tower has always been an icon of old docks.So it's situated near the site of the original Bertie's Landing Restaurant.

  • Was built in 1882 as the port captain's office.
  • The second floor of the Clock Tower is a decorative mirror room,so that he can check all the activities in the harbor.
  • Is now an important focal point in the Waterfront's urban design

Robinson Dry Docks

The Robinson Dry dock is situated in the Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.

  • Is the oldest operating dry dock of its kind in the world.
  • The foundation stone for the dock was laid by Prince Alfred.
  • Southern Africa’s first-ever dry dock.

Old Power Station

Old power station is currently a market on the Wharf

  • The original harbour area had electricity 10 years before the city.
  • The plant had a 200 horsepower (150 kW) engine and a dynamo with two boilers.
  • The power station supplied electricity to the Somerset Hospital, the Royal Observatory, the public library, the Houses of Parliament, the Old Somerset Hospital, the Cape Town Railway Station,and the Grand Hotel.
